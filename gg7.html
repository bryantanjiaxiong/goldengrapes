Golden Grapes

A Painting Of A New World

“Oh, hello there. Nice weather we’re having now. It has been nothing but storms lately.”

“Hm? What am I painting? Well, it’s still empty. But I’m trying to recreate this picture in my mind, you see, though I’m not really getting anywhere.”

“I wonder if today just might be the day I finally see it.”

 

“Do be careful not to step on that pile over there. They’re mine, yes, but they are…To put it bluntly, they are failures. But they’re still very precious to me, so please, do be careful.”

“No, no, it’s just that pile. I don’t have any good works yet; nothing at all to show you. But heavenly steel is forged from broken swords, so they say; that’s what I wish to achieve.”

“And the lost souls would gather to beckon grandeur anew. Perhaps if you wait a little longer…”

 

“You wish to know about the picture? The one in my mind? Oh, hm…I guess there’s no harm in telling someone. But how do I even begin? Do give me a moment.”

 

“Forgive me, I was lost in my thoughts. Perhaps…How about I start by…hm…”

“It’s a world of magic, essentially, magic of unpredictable behaviour, just like luck. A world where time passes through the era of wooden spears and spells to that of knights and angels then finally to the modern present, just like our world. Yes, along with its many faults: war and corruption and injustice, hunger and poverty, misery and tragedy.”

“But the world in my mind has to be beautiful, even with the pain it endures. Properly beautiful. That’s where my conundrum lies. How could one view suffering as beauty without lying to one’s self?”

“Hm, but leave it to me. I’ll figure it out. It’s my duty; I promised myself. An accord with one’s self is impossible to break, even if willingly.”

 

“Come again? You think there’s more to my story? I guess what I said was too conspicuously generic; you wish to know my proper definition of beauty: the overall distinction I’m finding difficult to portray. Well, I cannot deny it. Do you truly want to hear more?”

“I understand. This might be helpful to me as well. Admitting one’s ambitions aloud affirms one’s convictions.”

“There is hope to find order in chaos, to seek clarity within the depths. Even in our world, that cannot be proven false, for humanity persists. The only way for despair to be beautiful is if there is something after it, and this curiosity thickens. That is my sense of hope. I want to imbue that hope into my colours, to show our world the existence of possibilities by making my own world a simulated example.”

“I suppose that didn’t clear up as much as I’d hoped. Perhaps it would easier for me to explain if you ask some questions instead.”

 

“I see. How am I supposed to deliver that hope? I decided I would have a group of individuals represent my ideas. Protagonists. They will be some sort of flame in the painting; a torch held out for everyone in the world to see, whether in my world or this world which is ours.”

“I will have them embody the complexity and practicality of this hope, harness the lessons they have gained from The World’s Tale: the collective history; the various ages of wooden spears and knights and cannons, and so inspire an understanding in every heart and mind and soul. They must. The tides must be turned.”

“That is the flame I wish to paint.”

“That was…intense, wasn’t it? But it was still overly vague, I gather from your expression. Let’s try again then. Ask me another.”

 

“Why magic? The existence of magic in my world is like the existence of randomness in ours. Luck, but more solid. More structured. And sometimes more obedient.”

“In my painting, it will play the role of being captivating, to be something that distinguishes my world from our real one, to have those who view my painting be enchantingly lured into it and then leaving refreshed. Like light rain at a blazing dusk, the sunset colouring the liquid sparks. Such a scene, isn’t it? To grab their attention and to hold onto it.”

“It’s a simple reason, really, nothing much to elaborate on. And even if it’s showy, it is not at all the main message. It is also doing nothing to help you understand, so please continue on to the next question.”

 

“What do I want to realise? What kind of impact do I wish to make in reality? Evolution. Evolution in the mind. To convince our world that despite our innumerable differences and perspectives, we could search for the flexibility and understanding to evolve into a higher existence in unison.”

“And yes, we have tried countless times, but never through a flaming painting. I have to find a way to have my painting be a unique simulation of how it could be done, it being what could be.”

“This is my purpose. I don’t think I can devote my heart to anything else more deeply that I can for this. It’s what I believe only I could do. I have to make them understand, and then it could be done by everyone.”

“And finally, I would rest. I would sit and gaze at my painting and let time pass me by. That…Perhaps that is the true pinnacle of what I wish to realise.”

 

“Why is this my duty…? Hm…That was a slip of the tongue. But…Fine. Listen then.”

“For too long have I watched as our humanity is repeatedly used against us, and they who exploit us know not that they are being crippled as well, crippled by time. Everyone’s potential is robbed from them by time, by stagnancy, by unfounded contentment. We are prisoners of a misunderstood fate.”

“Ours is a world where happiness is still merely a temporary escape route to distract us from an unsatisfying reality…I cannot bear the thought of letting it just stay that way. I need to paint my heroes leading my world out from a similar predicament, to be a guide for the heroes of the real world to do the same.”

“I cannot help but feel that I owe it to those before me to try my own hand at it. Many have tried, and so I must too. Because, though they failed, the lessons they left behind are invaluable, and should I fail as well, I could only leave behind something as precious if and only if I give it my whole.”

“But…But before I fail, I want to try to succeed. So here I am, still hopeful and unwavering, endlessly curious about how all of it will go; how my painting will come to be, if it does.”

“You can’t tell me you’re not the least bit curious, can you now?”

 

“The more I say the more I seem naïve to you, do you deny it? You don’t need to hide it. No? You understand? Truly?”

“But you aren’t exactly sure how to feel either. I see. Sometimes, that sensation visits me too, letting me wonder if I actually know what I’m doing. I have doubts, but they make no matter; I’ve made up my mind.”

“It’ll not be easy to just give up anyway, especially now that I have confided in someone. I must thank you; now I’m surer than ever.”

“Will you await the day my painting is completed? Return to this place, I promise you, it will be spectacularly glorious.”




"Behold its size! This is my canvas. It's to be a cold, dark, and very gentle place. But first, I must see flame."
