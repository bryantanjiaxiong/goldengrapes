Golden Grapes

Stand Up

“Now as you all may know, I’m very pretentious. All talk and much to show, but nothing to actually prove. But that’s part of me, and I have entirely accepted it. There’s no use denying it, even though I’ll always be trying to tell people that I’m a special brand of garbage. Garbage is still garbage; and not only me, but some people know that really well too. You know what I mean?

*Faint laughter*

“Sometimes I think: at least I’m self-aware, right? Most people don’t even want to admit it. But as a wise man once said: ‘self-awareness does not absolve anybody of anything’. I don’t want to say who it is, but his name begins with a ‘B’.”

*Rippling laughter*

“No, no. No, it’s not me. Really, but it’s someone I really look up to. I’m not a wise man; I’m still a very young boy. A young pretentious boy. And there’s only two outcomes to being as pretentious as me: one, you actually break through and get properly acknowledged as a somebody, right? Very low chance, don’t go for it. Two: you go through a long phase of being an embarrassing laughingstock and eventually get used to it. We all know which road I’m heading down, eh?”

*Nervous laughter*

“Right? We know, right? We have the same answer in our minds, yes? No, keep it to yourself, you don’t need to share. Am I seeking pity by criticising myself? I don’t know. Is it really criticism? We’ll find out by the end of today; that’s why I’m here on this stage. I can’t help it; this is me being pretentious once more. Go on, clap.”

*Enthusiastic applause*

 

“Back when I was in school, I liked to think that I was known as The Master Of Washing His Hands, which has an acronym: F.R.E.A.K. You’d think ‘Wait, none of the letters match’, right? But that’s where you’re very mistaken. It very much matches, well, in the long run of things. You’ll see.”

*Uncertain laughter*

“That wasn’t very funny, was it? Fine, let me try again. The reason I’m called that was because: when I was washing my hands in the sink, I would give my very best effort in washing up to my elbows as high as I can. Just in case, right? And no, I must do it at least three times, just to be extremely safe – it must be three. Let me establish something first: the sink and the sink table must be disgusting, because everyone was using it, so it must be, alright? Never question it. Sometimes, when I was washing too…hm, how do I put it…rashly, there’d be water spills, splashing in random directions. And some of these bloody spills would bounce on the sink table back onto me and then I’d have to wash everything again. Do you get it? The clean water dropped onto the disgusting sink and became disgusting as well, bounced onto me and made me disgusting too. Get it? Are you sure you got it? No, wait, that doesn’t make much sense, does it? There are plenty of loopholes. Wait, I was doing it all wrong…Maybe I should’ve…What was the point? Ah, whatever, just to be the very safest, three more times.”

*Curious laughter*

“To avoid being called a brainless punk on top of being called a freak, it’s common courtesy to turn off the tap after I finish. But these taps, you see, how many others have touched them with their stinky fingers? So to all the brainless punks out there, I admire your conviction in doing what you believe in. I, a decent person albeit still a freak, had no choice but to submit to the social norm of closing taps, but in my own way. I had to scoop some water with one hand, sometimes both, and splash it onto the tap like I’m bathing the Buddha. And I must keep count. Six times, or at least six, just in case, and the final splash must neatly fall onto the tap without any rebounds, or else I would have to keep on splashing, with each failure adding another three times and then another. It must be in increments of three, no argument there. None, at all. A ridiculous scene, right? I remember losing my cool a few times: ‘Come on, just, come on, ugh, bloody stupid tap’. Bloody stupid me. Should have just left the water running, right? At least then nobody would care as much; they wouldn’t be staring.”

*Murmuring laughter*

“Yes, all while many groups of students are watching me. In the toilet, of course, only males, you don’t say. But in the school cafeteria, hoo boy, the pretty girls to impress! I wonder if they thought the way I was washing was me ruffling my peacock-feathers to them, like: ‘Look at me, I’m very clean’, but of course they’re disgusted. Though I never asked, I didn’t think I needed to. I couldn’t go: ‘Hey ladies, how was that?’. It was humiliating. But I told myself: ‘I doN’t CAre aBoUt tHeIR oPinIOnS, I nEed To tHInK foR mYsELf’. And so I spent, on average, three minutes at the sink, if I could be convinced by the silent stares to go fast enough, all the while thinking: ‘The only way to make this worth it is by telling a joke about it someday’. Attaboy. Mission complete.”

*Grand applause*

“Yes, you’re the first to hear about it, but it’s even more ridiculous to witness it. All my friends have it etched into their memory, an indelible stain on my reputation. But, don’t be disappointed, you could still get front-row seats to my next performance if you go to the second-floor toilet at five minutes past eleven. I promise, I’ll be there. No videos though; it’s an exclusive experience. ‘Behold the art of washing your hands’.”

*Rippling laughter*

 

“As you may have already guessed, I am diagnosed with Anxiety, or at least that’s the nearest term they found for me, alongside Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder and Pretending-to-be-super-clean Disorder. No, I made up that one. No, that last one. OCD is an actual thing. It’s what those people who like to arrange their pencils from shortest to longest claim they have. For me, I try to arrange water the best I can.”

*Semi-approving laughter*

“And as you already know, each attempt at water-arrangement takes close to five minutes. But that’s only for washing my hands. No one knows how I shower, you see. If they do, I would be known as The Master Of Showering. I don’t really like that title; it has too much of a ring than the previous one. Oh, how long do I shower? Take a guess, double it, and double it again, just in case. ‘But that’s half a day’, you would say. And you know what I would say? Double it! No, I’m just kidding. Or maybe I’m still too insecure to let people know my rightful title. That’s not important. Moving on to the joke, to the things I’m more comfortable joking about out loud.”

*Sympathetic laughter*

“My head has plans for how a ‘sImPLe’ shower must go, and if a few things don’t go well, my head would have direct counters prepared. Two things first: bugs and bubbles. That sounds like a band name, doesn’t it? If I see either of them, I would lose my marbles. Confused? You ought to be. Alright, think about this scenario: My legs are in need of some scrubbing, so I bend down and scrub, but then a bubble appears and pops in my face. Then finally I go: ‘Well screw you too’. Scenario ends. Now think: the bubble was definitely from my feet; I might have been careless and scrubbed too…rashly. My face was definitely much cleaner than my feet, but now they’re no longer very different. Because of the damn bubble. You don’t understand? I don’t expect common mortals to. I’m a Master Of Showering; I know what I’m doing. No, you listen. My feet are disgusting, so the bubble is disgusting, so my face is disgusting, so I need to spend one more hour on cleaning my face. Oh, woah there, look at your faces, all of you are thrown back in shock. What am I even saying? I don’t even know, it’s hard to explain, really. After all, it’s all in my head.”

*Disoriented-but-consistent laughter*

“Don’t even get me started on bugs. Bloody annoying creatures, spawning out of nowhere just to spite me. Use your heads and think, alright? It’s not that hard. Do it with me: the toilet bowl is disgusting, the water inside the toilet bowl is even more disgusting, the bug decides to go for a sip and becomes disgusting, it then decides to punch me in the face. Conclusion: My face becomes the toilet bowl. It’s not that hard to understand now, is it? Fine, the toilet bowl is an extreme case; it doesn’t always happen. But the floor in my bathroom is unarguably disgusting – it’s where the flowing water brings all the dirt to, right? It’s why people shower, to get dirt off them onto the floor. But the bugs hit the floor, and hit my face. My face becomes? The floor! Come on, it’s that simple. You mortals! Looks like I truly can’t get it across to you, doesn’t it? It’s stuck here; it’s all in my head. What can I do?”

*Rippling laughter*

“Wooh, I might have gone a bit too serious there. Anxiety is contagious. To lighten things up, here’s a quick joke: Bugs and Bubbles, a two-man band, and their promotional phrase: ‘If I see either of them, I would lose my marbles’. I’m not a big fan, really. But they’re quite persistent, always popping up in my shower, singing their favourite song: ‘Ten more hours! Ten more hours!’. I absolutely despise that song. Quite catchy though, so usually I cannot help but sing along.”

*Conclusive applause*

 

“So going to the toilet is like going to battlefield. Not even close, but…a mental battlefield, at least. A self-made eleven-star-difficulty mental battlefield. You might be thinking, how could a freak like me actually go outside, where there are more random creatures than bugs and bubbles? These creatures are sweaty, impulsive, casual, discourteous and overall: brainless punks. I’m not saying any of you are like them, but someone should. I’m kidding. I am saying exactly that. Can you mortals avoid me along the perimeter of a circle with a radius of one meter – just one? But maybe you all are terrible at geometry. You cannot compete with a freak that measures the trajectory of bubbles and waterdrops for a living, can you?”

*Relatable laughter*

“Walking on the street is a normal thing. Being afraid to walk on the street is bizarre. But before you judge, picture this: I’m walking with my friends, just minding my own bloody business, expecting to have great day, before a random drop of water flies onto my face from literally nowhere. I would look up and down and everywhere, my whole mind structure collapsing over one single drop of water. Where did this prick come from? How unlucky could I be that it lands on my face rather than a couple inches to the bloody left? Why not land on one of my friends’ face? They couldn’t care less, so why me? Is it from a pipe? What kind of pipe is it? Could it be rain? A miraculous one-drop rain that someone in a faraway desert needs more than I do? Is it someone’s sweat? I don’t want to taste it to find out. What if it’s spit? But there’s no one here, so maybe it’s from someplace high? God? Did you just spit on my day? Why would you do that? Why…Oh? For fun? I understand, haha, a very good day to you too. Alright, picture’s over, you can judge now. Go on, just don’t spit on me.”

*Tidal laughter*

“As much as I hate bubbles, sometimes I wish I could put myself in one, without the added inconvenience of preparing such a thing, of course. If there is one reason I want to be a wizard, it is to conjure up a bubble around me. Yes, something might glitch and I might end up floating up into the sky and get popped down, but sometimes when things go really wrong according to my ‘Stay Composed’ plans – which is fairly often, I can tell you that – I wouldn’t bloody mind. I would grateful for being saved from my head. No, I’m not what you’re thinking. I love living; I love my life. Really, you can see the truth on my face. I don’t actually know what’s the truth, but forget it. Back to the topic. What were we talking about? Oh yes, the outside…I shouldn’t have come back.”

*Expectable laughter*

But I have to go outside at some point, right? It’s the natural thing to do. For me, it sucks, but it is what it is. Life sucks the life out of everyone; deal with it. So I developed the hobby of seeing how much a day can screw me up as an individual. Don’t tell me you’re not curious. Not about me, I mean, but about you. About your day. My goal in life, if I have no choice but to continue to be a screwed-up freak: I want to know what time has in store for me. So I have to go on, right? No matter how much being such a freak is making other people uncomfortable. Whether or not I could fix my head and make myself and other people comfortable. Because of this curiosity, I have to wait and keep breathing. It’s like today. I was curious as to how this performance might end. Do you all remember that one question at the beginning? Yeah? No, shut up, I still don’t want to know. Not yet. But wait, shouldn’t I be wanting to know? What point am I trying to make? Hypocrite. Freak. We’ll find out. One day, I’ll make a joke while looking back at this performance. Pretentious or not, we’ll find out. Ah, look at the time, I better head back; can’t be late to my try-to-cry session. Please overlook my last desperate attempt at joking. Joking? Goodnight, everyone.

*Standing ovation*
