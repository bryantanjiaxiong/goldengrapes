Golden Grapes

Counselling By The Void

If you are talking with someone suicidal, it could be nerve-wracking, couldn’t it? There’s absolutely no right thing to say, with the extreme chance of everything you say being bloody wrong. The best person to talk to someone suicidal is themselves.

I do sometimes deal with people that…But I don’t understand. Wouldn’t they just repeat the same thing over and over again to their minds? To jump?

Don’t take your understanding exactly as it is from my sentence. What I mean is that any external voice is simply a catalyst to make them find themselves. Anyone that wishes to help has to allow this person to rediscover their reason to live, their humanity, by themselves.

Sure, but-

Of course, primarily, you can tell them the basic stuff: acknowledge that they are in their own indescribable pain, remind them of your all-time availability to be their listener, inform them that you care, tell them to have hope in their tomorrows and that you would check in regularly, whatever. But, you see, times are changing, and everyone’s minds are evolving to require higher maintenance. Soon, some other way must be found to cope with this compounding chaos.

 A professional can do much more than that, but that’s clearly not where you want to go with this. So…what other way is there?

Honesty. Absolute honesty. For sure, the person interacting with this suicidal person is on knife’s edge; one mistake is all it takes. Let them know that you are prone to say something wrong; let them know that you are being very careful with whatever words you are choosing to use; let them know that you want to help them so badly such that you are fraught with anxiety. Calmly, of course; it’s just to make them aware of your existence.

But then they wouldn’t be able to-

Rely on us? Wrong. If you really have to be worried, let them know that they can still rely on you. Let them know that there is still much to figure out, and that you can figure it out with them. Voice it aloud. Voice everything aloud. If you could get them to trust you with their emotions, you must do the same. Your empathy would seep over as theirs would to you. Let them synchronise with you, and then you must synchronise with them in turn. Let them talk to themselves. Like twin personas.

I don’t think…That doesn’t sound very practical.

It doesn’t, no, definitely not. But it is a sure-fire method. And I’m not done yet; here comes the tricky part: rekindle…Reignite their curiosity. Yes, ‘Our hope in our tomorrows’ is not exactly as it sounds. Not essentially. Sure, we would prefer the coming morning to fix everything up, or at least lessen the weight of the problems. But in all actuality, we want to find out what else might happen next. ‘How will everything go?’ is the constant question, the unquenchable thirst, the inextinguishable flame. It might fade sometimes, but what flame doesn’t occasionally need the extra breathing room to burn brighter?

Tricky…and risky. Every suicidal person is most likely fed up with their lives. It’s exactly why they…you know. They won’t be very curious.

And the flame wanes. So you must remind them to keep procrastinating their decision based on what the next second might bring. Fan the flame. No one asked to be made alive, but we are. It’s no simple thing to acknowledge, but we must.

I don’t get it.

‘How could this big mess be cleaned up?’, ‘How much worse can it get?’, ‘When is the next big mistake coming?’, ‘What is the next big mistake?’, ‘Will I get to escape this despair for some time before this next mistake?’, ‘How could happiness be reached again after all this sorrow?’, ‘Who will stay by my side?’, ‘Who will be the next to leave?’, ‘How will they leave?’, ‘Who will return to my side if and when things clear up?’, ‘What will be my salvation?’, ‘When will my salvation come?’, ‘Will I get to talk about everything one day in some healthy motivation speech?’. There are a billion questions, and a billion more is still not enough. If one dies, one will never find out. Let us all wait a little longer, what do you say? Isn’t that the very definition of living on?

I see. Tricky and risky indeed, but…a sure-fire way, huh? I understand, I think.

You understand? Truly? I talk a lot, but at least it got somewhere. Ha. Does that mean you’re ready?

I…Ready for what?

To rekindle my humanity.
